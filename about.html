About Patte Conservation

Learn about our mission, vision, and the dedicated community members working to conserve marine ecosystems.

Our Mission

To empower the Patte community to sustainably manage and conserve their marine resources through the establishment and effective management of the community conservation areas, improving livelihood, safeguarding biodiversity, and enhancing the overall well-being of the community.

Through collaborative management by the community of Pate, Shanga Ishakani and Shanga Rubu, we work to preserve coral reef health, promote biodiversity, and support sustainable livelihoods.

Our Vision

A thriving Patte community living in harmony with a healthy, resilient, and productive marine ecosystem.

We envision connected communities working together to conserve marine ecosystems while ensuring food security, economic development, and community well-being for future generations.

Our Core Values

These principles guide everything we do and shape how we work with marine conservation and community development.

Community-Led

We ensure that local community members are directly engaged in restoration activities and that their voices lead our initiatives.

Collaboration

We work hand-in-hand with communities from Pate, Shanga Ishakani and Shanga Rubu villages in joint marine conservation efforts.

Sustainability

We introduce sustainable usage of marine resources to address population growth while maintaining ecosystem health.

Partnership

We embrace partnerships with organizations like The Ocean Trust to share skills, knowledge, and tools for marine conservation.

Our Story

Community-driven marine conservation - the story of Patte Conservation.

Community Formation

Patte Conservation was formed as a Community-Based Organization (CBO) consisting of 10 dedicated members from three villages: 3 from Shanga Ishakani village, 3 from Shanga Rubu village, and 4 from Pate village. This collaborative initiative emerged from the shared need to conserve marine ecosystems.

Recognizing the Challenge

Our communities, which mostly depend on marine resources for their livelihoods, recognized that human population growth was increasing while marine production was decreasing. This realization drove us to seek sustainable solutions for resource management.

Partnership with Ocean Trust

We formed a vital partnership with The Ocean Trust Organization to enhance our coral reef restoration efforts. This collaboration ensures that restoration is community-driven, with local people at the heart of every activity.

Current Initiatives

Today, we actively manage the Iweni Locally Managed Marine Area (LMMA), focusing on coral restoration, environmental conservation awareness, and eco-tourism activities including snorkeling, diving, and sea adventures.

Future Vision

Our future plans include expanding advocacy on marine life conservation, mangrove restoration, and seaweed planting to create a more resilient and productive marine ecosystem for generations to come.
