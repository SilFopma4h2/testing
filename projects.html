Iweni Locally Managed Marine Area (LMMA)

Location: Patte, Shanga Ishakani, and Shanga Rubu villages, Kenya

Status: Active

Project Overview

Establishing and managing a community-driven Locally Managed Marine Area to foster sustainable and equitable marine ecosystem management. This initiative benefits both the environment and local communities through collaborative management.

Key Activities

  • Coral restoration activities
  • Environmental conservation awareness campaigns
  • Eco-tourism development (snorkeling, diving, sea adventures)
  • Sustainable fishing practice training
  • Community-based marine monitoring

Community Impact

  • 3 villages directly engaged
  • 10 CBO members leading initiatives
  • Local livelihoods supported through eco-tourism
  • Coral reef health monitoring and restoration

Coral Restoration Partnership

Key Partner: The Ocean Trust Organization

In partnership with The Ocean Trust Organization, our community has taken an active role in coral reef restoration efforts in Kenya.

Through this collaboration:

  • Local community members are directly engaged in restoration activities
  • Skills, knowledge, and tools are shared to empower people in marine conservation
  • Together, we are helping rebuild vibrant coral ecosystems that support biodiversity and livelihoods

Community Composition

  • Pate village: 4 members
  • Shanga Ishakani: 3 members
  • Shanga Rubu: 3 members

Future Plans

Planned initiatives to expand our marine conservation efforts.

Marine Life Advocacy

Location: Patte, Shanga Ishakani, and Shanga Rubu

Status: Planning Phase

Expanding advocacy programs to raise awareness about marine life conservation, sustainable fishing practices, and the importance of marine ecosystem preservation.

Focus on community education and stakeholder engagement to build broader support for marine conservation initiatives.

Mangrove and Seaweed Planting

Location: Coastal areas around the three villages

Status: Planning Phase

Large-scale mangrove restoration and seaweed planting programs to enhance coastal protection, improve water quality, and create additional sustainable livelihood opportunities for community members.

Includes training programs for sustainable harvesting and processing techniques.
