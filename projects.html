Iweni Locally Managed Marine Area (LMMA)

Location: Patte, Shanga Ishakani, and Shanga Rubu villages

Status: Active

Project Overview

Establishing and managing a community-driven Locally Managed Marine Area to foster sustainable and equitable marine ecosystem management. This initiative benefits both the environment and local communities through collaborative management.

Key Activities

  • Coral restoration activities
  • Environmental conservation awareness campaigns
  • Eco-tourism development (snorkeling, diving, sea adventures)
  • Sustainable fishing practice training
  • Community-based marine monitoring

Community Impact

  • 3 villages directly engaged
  • 10 CBO members leading initiatives
  • Local livelihoods supported through eco-tourism
  • Coral reef health monitoring and restoration

Partnership

Key Partner: The Ocean Trust Organization

Working alongside The Ocean Trust in joint coral reef restoration efforts, ensuring community-driven restoration with local people at the heart of every activity.

Community Composition

  • Pate village: 4 members
  • Shanga Ishakani: 3 members
  • Shanga Rubu: 3 members

Education for All

Location: South Asia (India, Bangladesh, Nepal)

Timeline: March 2022 - March 2026

Status: Active

Project Overview

Expanding access to quality primary education through school construction, teacher training, and educational resource development. Focus on reaching marginalized communities and ensuring gender equality in education.

Key Activities

  • Building and renovating schools
  • Training and supporting teachers
  • Providing school supplies and materials
  • Implementing digital learning programs
  • Supporting girls' education initiatives

Impact to Date

  • 12 schools established
  • 2,500+ students enrolled
  • 85 teachers trained
  • 500+ girls' scholarships provided

Project Funding

Goal: $400,000

Raised: $320,000 (80%)

Remaining: $80,000

Next Milestones

  • Q2 2025: Launch 3 new schools
  • Q3 2025: Digital learning expansion
  • Q4 2025: Teacher certification program

Healthcare Access Program

Location: West Africa (Ghana, Burkina Faso, Mali)

Timeline: June 2024 - June 2027

Status: Active

Project Overview

Improving healthcare access in rural communities through mobile clinics, community health worker training, and basic medical infrastructure development.

Key Activities

  • Operating mobile medical clinics
  • Training community health workers
  • Maternal and child health programs
  • Vaccination campaigns
  • Health education workshops

Impact to Date

  • 8 mobile clinics operational
  • 12,000+ patients treated
  • 60 health workers trained
  • 40 communities reached

Project Funding

Goal: $350,000

Raised: $200,000 (57%)

Remaining: $150,000

Next Milestones

  • Q2 2025: Launch 4 more clinics
  • Q3 2025: Maternal health focus
  • Q4 2025: Community health expansion
